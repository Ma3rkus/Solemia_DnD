## Layout
Walking through Solmaris, you will notice something strange. The town feels too big for the number of people living in it. Wide stone roads lead past half-empty buildings. Old statues stand weathered and forgotten. Grand halls remain locked or abandoned. Noble estates have become crowded housing or quiet ruins. Despite this, Solmaris is not dead. Markets still open each morning, farmers travel in from nearby villages, and some families proudly claim their ancestors helped build the Dominion itself.

Outside the town lie small farming villages, including Glenstead, a quiet settlement once home to the scholar [[Vaelor Kain]] before the events that changed history.

## History
Long ago, when the Dominion was first formed, the leaders of many regions gathered here to unite their lands. The city became a symbol of cooperation and early prosperity. But times change. When High Solis was built as the new capital, power slowly moved away from Solmaris. Nobles relocated, trade routes shifted, and government institutions followed the new center of power. Today, Solmaris is no longer a great city. It survives as a large town surrounded by the ruins of its former glory.

## People

The people who remain tend to be proud and nostalgic. Many still believe Solmaris is the true heart of the Dominion, even if the world has moved on. A common saying in town is:

> “High Solis rules the Dominion. Solmaris built it.”
