Snippets of the diary of [[Seraphine Crowe]]

## Snippet 1

Diary, 

I have avoided writing for several days. The others treat this like a heroic chronicle. I do not. These pages are for honesty. And the truth is this, I do not know if [[Vaelor Kain]] is entirely wrong. Before I met the others, I lived as a slave in Virelli. Chains. Markets. Ownership. Priests walked those streets. Temples stood on every corner. And yet the gods did nothing. [[Caldus Crowe]] believes the divine guides us. [[Gresha]] believes nature balances itself. [[Grindy]] believes problems can be solved with stew and optimism. I wish I could share their certainty. 

Vaelor’s crime is monstrous. But his question is not. Why do the gods decide what mortals must endure? Why must suffering be justified as "divine will"? If power exists, should it belong only to distant beings who never bleed? 

Tomorrow we confront him. [[Caldus Crowe]] believes we will save the world. I hope he is right. But I cannot stop thinking about the ritual we discovered. About the possibility he uncovered. About what it would mean if someone anyone could take the power of a god. Perhaps tomorrow we will destroy that possibility forever. 

Or perhaps… history will change.
