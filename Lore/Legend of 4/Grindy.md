## Tales
### Tale 1

The Legendary Four Heroes had been traveling through a mountain pass, but it has been blocked by a bandit fortress. Seraphine spent hours studying the fortress. Caldus suggested negotiating. Gresha wanted to wait until night. Grindy listened quietly, nodding along. Out of nowhere, Grindy pointed at a neaby cart full of barrels of goat fat. He said:

> “We could roll those downhill.”

Seraphine immediately said that the idea was reckless, loud, unpredictable, and very likely to fail. Grindry nodded and said, ofcouse ofcourse.... Then he rolled the barrels anyway. The barrels smashed into the fortress gate. The fat burst across the stone courtyard. And before the bandits could react, Caldus lit it with a single torch. The entire courtyard turned into a blazing sheet of fire. The bandits fled the walls. The gate burned open. And the fortress fell in less than ten minutes. Later that night, Seraphine reportedly said:

> “Your plan was catastrophically unsound.”

Grindry raised his mug and replied:

> “Ah, but it was a plan. HAHAHAHA.”

### Tale 2

This story is popular in the Stonewake Holds, especially when dwarves argue about strategy. The Four Heroes were once hunting a cave troll that had been killing miners. Seraphine laid out a careful strategy. They would lure the troll out of the cave. Trap it in sunlight. Then strike together. Grindry listened politely the entire time. When she finished explaining, he nodded. Then he walked directly into the cave. Caldus shouted after him. Gresha tried to stop him. Seraphine just stared in disbelief. A moment later the cave shook with roaring, crashing, and the unmistakable sound of a dwarf yelling. Dust poured from the cave entrance. Then Grindry walked out. Dragging the unconscious troll by one leg. Apparently he had thrown a cooking pot at its head and then wrestled it down the tunnel. Seraphine asked him why he ignored the plan. Grindry shrugged.

> “Your plan was good.”

Then he pointed at the troll.

> “But mine was faster.”
