# The Pact of the Turning Card

_A Contract of Fortune, Chance, and Wandering Roads_

Let it be known to all powers that watch the turning of fate, whether they dwell in forest shadow, wandering wind, silent grave, or laughing star, that this agreement is freely entered between:

**Lady Luck**, Keeper of Chance, Dealer of Fortune, Trickster of the Turning Paths, She Who Tips the Dice and Shuffles the Deck,

and

**Fintan Saoirse Fionn**, Walker of the Hidden Roads, Listener of the Forest, Bearer of Scars, Player of Dangerous Hands.

This pact is entered freely, without compulsion, under the eternal law that governs bargains between the unseen powers and those who dare accept their gifts.

---
## Article I — The Gift of Fortune
Lady Luck grants to Fintan Saoirse Fionn the following boons:

- The ability to bend chance at the edge of failure.
- Sight beyond sight, allowing instinct and unseen threads of fate to guide him where eyes cannot.
- Access to the subtle currents of magic carried within the unpredictable weave of fortune.
- The companionship of fate’s whisper, that gentle unseen influence which sometimes tilts the dice.

These powers shall not remain static. They shall grow and deepen as the bearer walks dangerous paths, accepts uncertainty, and continues to draw from the deck of destiny.

---
## Article II — The Path of the Wanderer
Fintan shall continue to walk the roads between forest, village, ruin, and wilderness.
He shall serve as a guide, guard, or companion to those who travel uncertain paths.
He shall place himself where risk gathers and where fate grows thin.
He shall oppose dangers that prey upon the helpless traveler.

Lady Luck does not demand heroism.
But she enjoys watching it unfold.

---
## Article III — The Law of the Gamble
When true chance presents itself, Fintan shall not refuse the gamble.
A gamble is defined as any moment in which fate stands uncertain and courage may influence the outcome. 

This may include:
- A dangerous decision with unclear result.
- A contest of skill or chance.
- A risk undertaken for another’s safety.
- A choice where fortune and ruin stand equally possible.

Fintan may choose how he plays such moments.
He may be clever, cautious, bold, or reckless.
But he must **play**.

To refuse the game entirely is to violate the spirit of this pact.

---
## Article IV — The Dance of Mischief
From time to time, Fintan shall upset the arrogance of those who believe themselves beyond consequence.

This may take the form of:
- harmless trickery,
- clever deception,
- the humiliation of tyrants,
- the unraveling of pride through unexpected fortune.

Cruelty is discouraged.
Embarrassment is encouraged.

For nothing delights Lady Luck more than watching the powerful discover that the game does not always favor them.

---
## Article V — The Balance of Fortune
Luck is not a river that flows only one direction.
Therefore the bearer of this pact must accept that fortune carries balance.
Great success may be followed by hardship.
Victory may be followed by danger.
A streak of impossible fortune may someday draw a losing card.

Lady Luck reserves the right to rebalance the game should the story grow dull.
This clause exists for the sake of entertainment.

---
## Article VI — The Instrument of Chance
The conduit of this pact shall take the form of a **Deck of Fortune**, a set of enchanted cards bound to the soul of the agreement.

The Deck shall contain fifty-four cards, divided into four suits representing the forces that shape destiny:
- **Hearts** — endurance, patience, survival
- **Clubs** — conflict, pain, sacrifice
- **Spades** — movement, travel, unpredictability
- **Diamonds** — mystery, magic, hidden truths

The face value will determine the effect:

| Face  | Effect                                                                                                      |
| ----- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 2     | Spell fails; slot expended. Cast a known cantrip; draw a new card for effect.                               |
| 3     | Gains Concentration; previous Concentration ends. Healing requires Con save to maintain.                    |
| 4     | Spell cast at lowest level; discard next top card along with this one.                                      |
| 5     | Halve damage dice; cannot cast again until next long rest. Another 5 ends this effect.                      |
| 6     | Deck handed to DM; top six cards may be reordered or discarded; one Heroic Inspiration gained if discarded. |
| 7     | Bearer becomes additional target; if already affected, nearest unaffected creature targeted.                |
| 8     | Dash or Disengage as part of casting; up to four cards may be discarded from top or bottom.                 |
| 9     | Replace with unknown Warlock spell; examine bottom six cards; discard some; shuffle.                        |
| 10    | Swap places with one target within 120 feet; if only target, draw again and discard both cards.             |
| Jack  | Examine bottom five cards; discard any number; remaining shuffled atop deck.                                |
| Queen | Double damage dice; discard three chosen cards and replace from discard pile (excluding Queens/Aces).       |
| King  | Triple damage dice; discard all of one Face Value; reset on reshuffle.                                      |
| Ace   | Regain a Pact Magic slot; cannot be returned by ordinary means. All four reshuffle when discarded.          |
| Joker | Choose Suit; roll d12; treat as face value (Aces=1, Jacks=11, Queens=12, no Kings); shuffle back.           |
Each card bears an arcane signature, reflecting Lady Luck’s capricious will.

Cards may not be destroyed, duplicated, or removed from the bearer’s possession except by the will of Lady Luck.

Two additional cards shall exist within the deck, both known as **The Fool**, symbolizing Lady Luck herself.
Each card shall bear unique carvings which the bearer may recognize by touch alone.
The Deck shall serve as the arcane focus through which Lady Luck’s magic flows.

---
## Article VII — The Practice of the Cards
When invoking the powers granted by this pact, the bearer may draw upon the Deck of Fortune in the following ways:

**The Draw**  
A single card may be drawn and used to channel magic. The card may be cast like a blade, placed upon the earth, or released into the air to awaken arcane effects.

**The Shuffle**  
The act of shuffling the deck aligns the bearer’s mind with the unpredictable rhythm of fate, allowing intuition and instinct to guide decisions.

**The Spread**  
By laying the cards upon a surface and tracing them with his fingers, the bearer may sense faint whispers of fortune, guiding his judgment in uncertain moments.

The cards shall always return to the deck after the magic is spent.

Loss of the deck does not end the pact, but separation from it causes the magic to grow faint and distant.

---
## Article VIII — The Behavior of the Deck
Cards may shift, appear unexpectedly, or arrange themselves in response to important moments of fate.

This behavior reflects the subtle amusement of Lady Luck and does not constitute malfunction.

---
## Article IX — The Twin Fools
Two cards within the deck bear the symbol of the Fool.

The Fool represents Lady Luck herself and the unpredictable nature of fortune.
One Fool represents impossible success, miraculous survival, and outrageous victory.

The other represents failure, humiliation, and painful lessons.
The bearer shall not always know which has been drawn.

Lady Luck considers this detail extremely amusing.

---
## Article X — Duration of the Pact
This pact shall remain in effect for the duration of Fintan Saoirse Fionn’s mortal life, unless Lady Luck herself dissolves the agreement or the bearer willingly abandons the game forever.

Lady Luck does not expect the latter to occur.

---
## Article XI — The Final Game
When the life of the bearer reaches its final turning, when the last road has been walked and the final breath approaches,

Lady Luck shall claim one final wager.

What form that wager takes shall be determined at that moment.
It may involve cards, dice, riddles, chance, or some other game yet unimagined.
The outcome of that final game will determine the closing chapter of the bearer’s story.

Lady Luck is greatly looking forward to it.

---
## Signatories
This pact is sealed by chance itself and acknowledged by the unseen forces that witness all bargains between mortals and the powers beyond.

**Lady Luck**  
Dealer of the Unpredictable Hand  
Keeper of the Turning Card

**Fintan Saoirse Fionn**  
Walker Between Fortune and Ruin  
Bearer of the Deck of Fortune
