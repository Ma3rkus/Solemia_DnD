# Cliodhna

The Cliodhna is the principal military structure of the woodland elves, though calling it an army would be misleading. It is better understood as a sacred martial order bound by oath to protect the forests, ancient sites, and the balance between the mortal world and the spirit realm.

The order traces its name to Cliodhna, an ancient elven queen and war-seer who lived during the early ages of the woodland kingdoms. Legends claim she could hear the voices of spirits carried by the wind and foresaw the destruction of entire clans before the enemies even reached their borders. She organized the scattered woodland defenders into a disciplined force, combining warfare, spiritual guardianship, and covert operations.

Because of this heritage, the Cliodhna is structured differently than most militaries:
- Wardens – frontline protectors and rangers guarding territory
- Seers – spiritual advisors and omen readers
- Shades – covert operatives working outside traditional battlefields

The order rarely marches in formation. Instead, it fights through ambush, knowledge of the land, and supernatural awareness. Many outsiders doubt the Cliodhna even exists, believing it to be merely a collection of hunters and forest guardians.

Within elven society, membership is both honor and burden. Members swear an oath not only to their people but also to the ancient spirits of the forest. Breaking this oath is considered worse than exile.

Because of the secrecy of certain divisions, even those trained within the order—like Fintan—may know only fragments of its deeper structure.

---
## The Guard of the Banshee (Elite Division)

Among the hidden branches of the Cliodhna, none are spoken of with more reverence or unease than the Guard of the Banshee.

This elite unit specializes in reconnaissance, counter-assassination, and supernatural threats. They operate where the boundaries between the mortal world and the spirit world grow thin.

The name comes from the belief that when the Guard moves through the forest, their enemies die before they even realize they were being hunted, much like those who hear the wail of a banshee only moments before death.

Members of the Guard are marked with a ritual tattoo:

- The mark is burn-carved into the skin using wood from the Crann Bethadh (Tree of Life).
- The pain of the ritual symbolizes the death of one identity and the birth of another.
- Only those who complete training survive the ritual without permanent madness.

Members are trained to rely on senses beyond sight, including sound, vibration, scent, and spiritual awareness. Because of this, some believe Fintan’s blindness may have made him uniquely suited for their training.

Guard operatives often work alone or in small cells. Their missions typically include:

- Eliminating threats before they reach elven lands
- Tracking enemies across vast territories
- Investigating disturbances among spirits and ancient places
- Gathering intelligence through covert networks

Few outside the order know the Guard even exists. Among those who do, they are sometimes called _“the Whispering Dead.”_

## Craobh Ruadh (The Secret Branch)

The Craobh Ruadh—translated loosely as _The Red Branch_ is an extremely secretive special operations unit whose existence is denied even within the Cliodhna.

Its name comes from ancient warrior legends describing a band of heroes who defended the realm during the earliest ages. Whether the modern Craobh Ruadh directly descends from those legends is unclear.

Recruitment is rare and highly selective. Candidates receive a Raven Seal, an invitation rather than a command. Accepting the seal means agreeing to disappear from official records for the duration of their service.

The Craobh Ruadh handles missions considered too sensitive or dangerous for other units, including:

- Political assassinations to prevent wars
- Eliminating supernatural entities threatening the balance of the forest
- Recovering powerful artifacts before they fall into the wrong hands
- Covert operations beyond elven territories

Members are trained to operate alone behind enemy lines, sometimes for years.

Even among the Cliodhna, many believe the Craobh Ruadh is merely a legend used to frighten recruits into discipline. Those who know the truth rarely speak of it.

Fintan’s role as a tracker candidate suggests he possessed rare skills in sensing movement, identifying trails, and navigating terrain without conventional sight—abilities highly valued by the unit.

His recall before completing any missions may have saved him from a life where returning home would have been impossible.

# Allies - NPCs

## 1. Tuatha Dé Danann (Commander of the Guard)

Tuatha Dé Danann is not her birth name but a title granted to her after decades of service. The title connects her symbolically to the mythic heroes of ancient elven legend.

She is known within the Cliodhna as a master of silent warfare and spiritual intuition. Stories claim she once tracked a rogue druid across three kingdoms without ever leaving a visible trail.

Her leadership style is calm, distant, and precise. She rarely raises her voice and believes that the most effective weapon is knowledge.

Key traits often attributed to her:

- Exceptional tracker and strategist
- Deep connection with the spirit world
- Known for training unconventional recruits
- Possesses an unsettling ability to sense deception

It was Tuatha Dé Danann who oversaw Fintan’s training. Many within the Guard questioned the decision to train a blind elf, but she reportedly said:

> “The forest does not see with eyes. It listens.”

Under her guidance, Fintan learned to refine his heightened senses until he could move and fight almost indistinguishably from sighted warriors.

Despite her reputation, very little is known about her personal life. Some rumors claim she has served the Cliodhna for centuries beyond a normal elven lifespan, possibly through unknown magical means or spiritual pacts.

## 2. Maerwen “The Still Wind”

**Role:** Senior reconnaissance operative
Maerwen is known for an unsettling ability to move without disturbing even the smallest branch or leaf.

Other Guard members claim she once stood in the middle of a battlefield unnoticed for an entire hour simply by remaining perfectly still.

Personality:
- Quiet
- Observant
- Dry sense of humor

Relationship with Fintan:
She was one of the few who defended Tuatha Dé Danann’s decision to train him.
She once told him:
> “Most soldiers rely too much on their eyes. You were forced to learn the forest’s real language.”

---
## 3. Branach “Three-Scars”

**Role:** Veteran hunter of supernatural threats
Branach earned his name after surviving an encounter with a forest wraith that left three claw marks across his face.

He is large for an elf and favors brutal, efficient combat rather than subtlety.

Personality:
- Direct
- Loyal
- Distrustful of outsiders

Relationship with Fintan:

At first he believed training a blind elf was foolish.
But after witnessing Fintan track a target through rainfall and across stone, Branach changed his opinion.

His compliment was simple:
> “You hunt like winter.”

Among the Guard, this is extremely high praise.

# 4. Eithne “Whisperleaf”

**Role:** Information broker for the Guard
Eithne rarely participates in direct combat.

Instead, she manages the network of informants, travelers, smugglers, and spies who pass information to the Guard.

She is one of the few who teaches [[Thieves' Cant]] to recruits.

Personality:

- Charming
- Manipulative
- Always smiling

Relationship with Fintan:
She was the one who first taught him to listen to speech patterns instead of facial expressions.

She often says:
> “Eyes lie. Voices don't.”

She might still recognize him even under a new name.
