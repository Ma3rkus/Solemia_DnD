# House of Fionn
#lore #Family 

House Fionn is known across Coill Mhór and parts of Aird an Teine not for ruling, but for guiding events quietly.  
Their influence comes from three traditions:

- Master craftsmanship of elven longbows
- Diplomatic mediation between woodland communities
- A deep spiritual respect for the forest

The family rarely seeks public power, yet many political decisions in Evergreen have quietly passed through their counsel.

## Generation I — The Grandparents

### Caelan Fionn — “The Quiet Cartographer”
**Family role:** Grandfather
Role: Territorial advisor and forest strategist

Caelan Fionn never served as a soldier, yet he was frequently consulted by the wardens of the Cliodhna when territorial disputes arose.

He possessed a rare ability to understand the forest as a living system rather than a collection of locations.

He could determine:

- safe travel routes
- animal migration patterns
- hidden paths used by smugglers or spies

Because of this knowledge, several communities relied on him to resolve disputes over land and hunting territory.

#### Personality
- Analytical
- Extremely patient
- Guarded with information

He believed knowledge should be shared carefully and only when necessary.
His philosophy:

> “A forest path is safest when only those who need it know it exists.”
### Liathwen Ó Daire — “Keeper of Voices”
**Family role:** Grandmother

Liathwen came from a long line of songkeepers and oral historians.

Her duty was to preserve stories, events, and agreements between woodland settlements.  
Many treaties in Evergreen were remembered through her recitations rather than written record.

She possessed an extraordinary memory and could repeat conversations word-for-word decades later.
#### Personality
- Charismatic
- Sharp-witted
- Warm but perceptive

Liathwen often balanced Caelan’s seriousness with humor and storytelling.

She ensured that the House of Fionn remained connected not only to politics and craft but also to culture and memory.

## Generation II — The Parents
### Finnbar Fionn
**Family role:** Father

Finnbar became one of the most respected longbow craftsmen in Coill Mhór.
His bows were famous for:
- near-silent draw
- exceptional durability
- balance suited for woodland combat

Despite this, Finnbar rarely sold his finest bows to outsiders.  
Instead he preferred to equip local hunters and wardens who protected the forest.

#### Personality
- Proud
- Traditional
- Strong sense of duty

When Fintan fell into disgrace, Finnbar supported the family’s decision to cast him out.
However, he secretly monitored his son’s survival through quiet contacts.
Before his death he restored Fintan’s honor by commissioning the mask Foscadh na Crann.
This act was both forgiveness and acknowledgement that Fintan had endured his trials.
### Sinead Ui Riain
**Family role:** Mother

Sinead came from a respected herbalist family within Evergreen.
She possessed deep knowledge of healing plants, fermentation, and woodland medicine.
She was responsible for teaching all her children the basics of:
- herbal medicine
- food preservation
- brewing techniques

Ironically, this tradition is what later influenced Fintan’s love of brewing, though his reckless experimentation often led to dangerous results.
#### Personality
- Gentle but firm
- Practical
- Protective of her children

Unlike Finnbar, she never believed Fintan was beyond redemption.

## Generation III — The Children of House Fionn
### Feanan Fionn
**Family role:** Older Brother

Feanan inherited the diplomatic instincts of the family.
Rather than becoming a craftsman, he works as a liaison between forest settlements and regional leaders in Aird an Teine.
#### Personality
- Calm
- Strategic
- Skilled negotiator

He understands how to move through politics quietly.

Feanan rarely speaks about Fintan, but he is known to have defended his brother privately during family discussions.
### Fithgen Fionn
**Family role:** Younger Brother

Fithgen followed directly in his father’s footsteps.
He now works in the family workshop crafting longbows and hunting weapons.
Unlike Finnbar, he is more open to experimentation and sometimes works with foreign woods and hybrid designs.
#### Personality
- Curious
- Friendly
- Slightly rebellious toward tradition

He admired Fintan as a child and struggled deeply with the family’s decision to exile him.
### Fonora Fionn
**Family role:** Younger Sister

Fonora became a warden of the outer forest paths, helping patrol the trade routes leading into Coill Mhór.
She is one of the few members of the family who actively works alongside the Cliodhna wardens.
#### Personality
- Fierce
- Loyal
- Protective of the forest

She respects Fintan’s strength but resents the pain his actions caused their parents.
