## Polite Insults
(Quiet, dry, often said once and never repeated)
### Soft, Civilized Cuts

- “You speak with great confidence for someone standing on borrowed ground.”
- “Ah. I see. You’ve mistaken volume for weight.”
- “That’s an opinion. Not a sturdy one, but still.”
- “Interesting logic. It breaks easily.”
- “I’ll remember that—for when it stops mattering.”

### To the Arrogant / Self‑Important

- “Power really does hide the cracks, doesn’t it?”
- “Careful. Pride carries poorly in the wind.”
- “You seem very sure. Fate enjoys that.”
- “You remind me of a tall tree with shallow roots.”
- “Ard ach lag.” (ARD akh lag) — “Tall, but weak.”

### Quiet Mockery

- “I’ve heard sharper thoughts from safer people.”
- “That was almost impressive.”
- “You’re certain. That’ll be educational.”
- “Please—carry on. I like knowing who I’m dealing with.”
- “The forest would have corrected you by now.”

### Veiled Warnings (Sound Like Advice)

- “There’s still space to walk this back.”
- “This path ends badly. For someone.”
- “Luck’s drifting away from you.”
- “We’re very close to a lesson.”
- “Tá an ghaoth ag casadh.” (Taw un gwee ag KOSS-uh) — “The wind is turning.”
### Dry, Blink‑and‑Miss‑It Humor

- “That was… enthusiastic.”
- “Bold choice. Not a clever one.”
- “You seem committed. That’s unfortunate.”
- “I admire the courage. The thinking less so.”
- “Yes. That explains quite a lot.”

### Signature Shutdowns

- “I think we’re done.”
- “No. Try again—more quietly.”
- “You’ve said enough.”
- “This conversation has peaked.”
- “Éist.” (AY-sht) — “Listen.”
