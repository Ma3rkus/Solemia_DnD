# RP guide

## ROLEPLAY GUIDE
### 1. BELIEFS
> (what he genuinely believes, not what he claims aloud)


*The world is a living system, not a battlefield.*
Forests, people, spirits, and even politics behave like ecosystems. Push too hard in one place and rot spreads elsewhere.
> “Gearrtar an brainse, ach maireann an fhréamh.”
(GYAR-tar un BRAN-sheh, akh MAIR-yan un RAY-uv)
— You can cut the branch, but the root endures.


*Luck is neither kind nor cruel — it is participatory.
Fate is like a poorly shuffled deck: biased against those who refuse to draw.*
Fintan believes chance demands engagement. Standing aside when the cards are on the table is the only true sin.
He does not pray for luck. He answers it.


*Power must be tested to remain legitimate.
Untested authority grows soft, then poisonous — like fruit left too long in the sun.*
He prefers embarrassment, exposure, or misdirection. Violence is a last card, played face‑down.


*Redemption is conditional and endlessly provisional.
No one is “done.”* 
> Including him.
Redemption is not a destination; it is a path (cosán — KOH-sawn) that must be walked again every morning.


*Silence preserves more than speech ever could.
Words scatter like birds. Silence takes root.*
Or as his grandmother would say:
> “Ní insítear an fhírinne don ghaoth.”
(Nee IN-sher-tyer un EE-rin-yeh don gwee)
— You don’t tell the truth to the wind.

### 2. BASE TRUTHS
> (what is always true, whether others know it or not)

*He is blind — functionally and socially.*
Functionally, blindness sharpened him into something else: sound becomes shape, movement becomes geometry, luck becomes texture.
Socially, people underestimate him. He lets them. Underestimation is a gift; refusing it would be rude.

*He was shaped by elite, oath‑bound discipline.*
The Cliodhna carved patience into his bones. He learned to wait like winter waits — inevitable, quiet, and deeply inconvenient.
He denies membership not out of shame, but habit. Old oaths echo if spoken aloud.

*Lady Luck is his patron, not his leash.*
The pact is a game, and he knows better than to confuse table rules with ownership.
The Deck is not a focus; it is a conversation. Sometimes an argument.

*He is House Fionn by temperament, not ambition.*
He moves events without signing them.
He opens doors and leaves before anyone thinks to ask who unlocked them.

*He belongs on roads, thresholds, and crossings.*
Inns, borders, forest paths, half‑remembered trails.
Destinations expect too much permanence.

### 3. SECRETS
> (what actually moves him, beneath discipline and jokes)


*Loss of autonomy frightens him more than death.*
Slavery, addiction, betrayal — all different masks for the same thief.
This is why he favors risk taken freely over safety imposed.


*He expects fortune to balance its books.*
Long streaks of good luck make him uneasy. He starts counting exits.
Bad times feel honest. At least they aren’t pretending to like him.


*He trusts Lady Luck’s rules — not her motives.*
He suspects a card yet unturned.
She suspects this awareness is what makes him interesting.
Both smile. Neither relaxes.


*Distance from family is protection, not bitterness.
Love is leverage to enemies who know how to squeeze.
Better to be a wandering story than a fixed target.*


*He does not believe peace is owed to him.*
Rest is a pause between shuffles.
The final wager is coming. He intends to be awake when it arrives.



## COMBAT GUIDE (+ MENTAL REMINDERS)
### 1. COMBAT MINDSET

- Combat is already happening before blades are drawn.
- Chaos is terrain — fog, wind, uneven ground.
- Dying gloriously is still dying stupidly.

### TACTICAL PRINCIPLES

- Cut keystones, not bricks.
- Trade safety for position, never for pride.
- When luck misbehaves, invite it closerProtecting others preserves future moves.

#### WHEN IT GOES WRONG

- No panic. No anger.
Just a quiet recalculation.
> “Bhuel.” (VYUL) — the Gaelic equivalent of “Well… that’s unfortunate.”

#### MENTAL REMINDERS (COMBAT)

> Is tír í an chaos.
(Iss TEER ee un KHA-os)
— Chaos is a country; learn its roads.

- You only need to win later.

## DEFAULT REACTIONS

*Threatened:* stillness → listening → precise action

*Insulted:* silence → dry comment → lets fate reply

*Praised:* redirects credit (“Luck was in a good mood.”)

*Challenged about past:* reframes, then closes the door

*Clear injustice:* intervenes, grumbling softly afterward

*True gamble:* accepts immediately — hesitation would offend the table


## VOICE & MANNERISMS
### VOICE

Low, even, unhurried
Pauses are intentional, not searching

### SPEAKING STYLE

Forest and card metaphors come naturally
Humor dry enough to miss if you blink
Rare lies; frequent omissions

### MANNERISMS

Head tilts when listening — like a bird before flight
Fingers walk the deck edges when fate stirs
Touches mask or neck tattoo only when old paths resurface

### EMOTIONAL TELLS

Overload → fewer words
Nostalgia → softer tone
Surprise → brief, genuine smile
(Always gone too soon.)


## MICRO CHEAT LINES
*Universal:*
“Listen. Pause. Act.”

*Social:*
“Let them play their hand first.”

*Danger:*
“Luck favors motion.”

*Moral choice:*
“Which path keeps choice alive?”

*Private mantra:*
“Ní thagann an ghaoth gan chúis.”
(Nee HAH-gun un gwee gun khoos)
— The wind doesn’t rise without a reason.

## RELATIONSHIP STANCES
*Allies* — quiet loyalty, shared risk, no spotlight
*Strangers* — polite, guarded, listening
*Authority* — compliant mask, skeptical core
*The Arrogant* — tests them gently… at first
*The Helpless* — immediate action, no speeches
*Enemies* — impersonal, efficient, final when needed
