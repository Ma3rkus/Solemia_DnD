## General / Everyday Lines

- “Give it a moment. The world usually explains itself if you stop interrupting it.”
- “I don’t need to see it. I need to hear what it’s doing.”
- “Paths show themselves once you stop arguing with them.”
- “Luck’s circling. You can feel it, yes?”
- “Silence first. Words later. That order matters.”

## Dry Humor (understated, blink‑and‑you‑miss‑it)

- “Ah. This seems… poorly shuffled.”
- “Good news: we’re alive. Bad news: fate noticed.”
- “Yes, that was the plan. It was a bad plan, but still.”
- “I’ve survived worse decisions than this. Not many, but a few.”
- “Luck has a sense of humor. It’s not a kind one.”

## When Assessing a Situation

- “Let’s see which way the wind’s lying.”
- “Something’s off. Can’t tell you what yet. I’ll know when it moves.”
- “This place remembers things. Not kindly.”
- “Still. Listen. There — did you hear that pause?”

## Advice (Quiet, Non‑Authoritative)

- “Choose the road that leaves you options.”
- “Don’t rush. That’s how the ground decides for you.”
- “If you must act, act cleanly.”
- “Nothing wrong with walking away. Even the forest sheds leaves.”
- “Cosán mall, teach slán.” (KOH-sawn moll, tyakh slawn) — “A slow path leads home safe.”
## On Luck, Fate, and Gambling

- “Luck doesn’t need belief. Only participation.”
- “Refusing the draw is still a choice.”
- “Fate favors motion, not courage.”
- “When the cards feel warm, something’s about to be honest.”
- “Ní thagann an ghaoth gan chúis.” (Nee HAH-gun un gwee gun khoos) — “The wind doesn’t rise without a reason.”

## To Allies

- “I’ll walk point. Shout if I don’t come back.”
- “Stay close. Not because it’s safer — because it’s quieter.”
- “If things turn ugly, follow the sound, not the shouting.”
- “You did well. Don’t argue — just take it.”

## To Strangers

- “Evening. You look like someone who hears answers before questions.”
- “No need for names yet. Let’s see how this unfolds.”
- “Careful — you’re standing where stories tend to trip.”

## To Authority or the Powerful

- “Of course. Your rules make perfect sense.” (they do not)
- “Power’s a heavy cloak. Easy to trip over.”
- “I’m sure this arrangement benefits someone. Let’s find out who.”
- “Is tréimhse í an chumhacht.” (Iss TRAY-im-she ee un KOO-akt) — “Power is only a season.”

## To the Arrogant

- “Confidence is useful. Certainty is dangerous.”
- “Careful — the world enjoys proving people wrong.”
- “You’re very loud for someone standing on thin ground.”
- “Let’s see how fortune feels about you.”

## To the Helpless or Afraid

- “Easy now. You’re not alone on this road.”
- “Breathe. The ground’s still here.”
- “I’ve got you. Look at me — or don’t. Either works.”
- “You kept moving. That’s enough for now.”

## Warnings & Threats (Never Raised Voice)

- “You should stop. This path ends badly.”
- “Last chance to turn aside. I won’t ask again.”
- “Luck’s already leaning away from you.”
- “Éist.” (AY-sht) — “Listen.”

## In the Middle of Danger

- “Now. Before the moment notices us.”
- “Left. No — your other left.”
- “Still. Still— now.”
- “That was the wrong card.”

## After Things Go Wrong

- “Bhuel.” (VYUL) — “Well…”
- “That could have gone worse.”
- “We’re still drawing breath. Let’s not waste it.”
- “Next time, we shuffle first.”

## Quiet, Poetic, or Introspective

- “Some roads only exist while you’re walking them.”
- “Scars remember better than minds.”
- “The forest doesn’t forgive. It balances.”
- “I don’t fear endings. I fear unfinished games.”
- “Fanann an rian.” (FAN-un un REE-an) — “The trace remains.”

## Signature Exit Lines

- “I’ll walk a bit longer. You’ll hear if I need you.”
- “Paths diverge here. Good luck — and mean it.”
- “Try not to anger fate. It gets bored easily.”
- “If we meet again, the cards will explain why.”
